--- layout: post title: Fixing blank pages after upgrading to WordPress 3 tags: - All - Code status: publish type: post published: true meta: aktt_notify_twitter: 'yes' _edit_last: '1' _syntaxhighlighter_encoded: '1' aktt_tweeted: '1' _wp_old_slug: '' _sexybookmarks_shortUrl: http://b2l.me/axb56j _sexybookmarks_permaHash: 29b34dd917299030e2c952c459d3850d --- Today I finally clicked the auto-upgrade button to upgrade my WordPress site to the newest version, 3.0.1. I then noticed that I wasn't seeing the content of a lot of my pages. I could still see the lists of posts but I wasn't getting anything on a single post or normal page. I verified the data was in the database. Here's what my page.php looked like: {% highlight php %} <?php get_header(); ?> <div id="pageWrapper"> <h2><?php the_title(); ?></h2> <?php the_content(); ?> </div> <?php get_footer(); ?> {% endhighlight %} This was all fine and dandy for WordPress 2.x but obviously wasn't working for 3.0. Turns out the solution is quite simple. I just need to put the_content() back into "the loop": {% highlight php %} <?php get_header(); ?> <div id="pageWrapper"> <?php if (have_posts()) : while (have_posts()) : the_post(); ?> <h2><?php the_title(); ?></h2> <?php the_content(); ?> <?php endwhile; endif; ?> </div> <?php get_footer(); ?> {% endhighlight %} I'm not sure why the change but I suppose I should have always explicitly used "the loop". Maybe I was taking advantage of a bug this whole time? Or maybe this was an intentional change that I should have seen coming. At any rate, if you see your content missing after an upgrade, take a look at your theme and make sure the_content() is always inside "the loop".
